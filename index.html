Why AI Product Management?

It started with Netflix. During my undergraduate studies at Beijing Film Academy, my professor led a deep analysis of Netflix's evolution — from DVD rentals to a data-driven content empire. That was the moment I realized: the future of media isn't just creative — it's technical. I decided then that my edge would be the ability to bridge content and technology.

Then AI arrived — faster than I expected. While preparing for graduate school, generative AI exploded. ChatGPT, Sora, Stable Diffusion — the intersection I'd been drawn to was suddenly the center of the industry. I didn't pivot to AI. AI came to me.

I started hands-on: training LoRA models and building AIGC pipelines at Enlight Media (the studio behind Ne Zha 2). Then I moved deeper into product — defining the vision for a multimodal AI companion at Zhipu AI, shipping features to 1,500+ beta testers. At Tianji Cloud, I independently built and launched an AI-native consumer app. Now at USC's AI Trust Lab, I'm tackling the harder question: how do we make AI systems trustworthy enough for enterprise adoption?

What I bring that most PM candidates don't: I come from a world where story, aesthetics, and audience experience are everything. Most PMs optimize for metrics. I optimize for metrics and for how the product feels. At USC and Tsinghua, I'm now building the technical foundation — machine learning, deep learning, data science — so I can speak both languages fluently: the language of engineering, and the language of human experience.

The thread connecting all of it: I don't just want to build AI products that work — I want to build AI products that people trust, enjoy, and remember.

A preparation system, not a question generator — structured role-specific training that improves candidates across an entire interview cycle.

01
Fragmented prep — resume, research, questions, advice all disconnected. No system.

5-Agent Architecture
Loop Preparation Loop
  • Background Builder — persistent digital profile from resume & context
  • Role Context Agent — job-specific criteria from JD input
  • Interview Orchestrator — adaptive pacing, difficulty, follow-ups
  • Scoring Agent — 6-dimension evaluation with role-adjusted weights
  • Progress Tracker — feeds weaknesses into next simulation
OKR Success Metrics

North Star — Score improvement across 3 consecutive mock sessions

  • Mock completion rate · 7-day return rate
  • Sessions per Target Role · subscription conversion
  • Weakness convergence rate
± Tradeoffs
  • Deep personalization → modular agents + clean data flows to avoid brittle chains
  • Adaptive orchestration → balance realism with response speed
  • Video analysis deferred → prioritize structured text scoring in MVP
  • Positioning risk → must emphasize growth, not just another mock tool
